## The reframe

Not a cosmological claim. A phenomenological reframe.

The standard cognitive stance treats the past as knowable-but-sparse (we remember what we remember, with gaps we know we have) and the future as speculative-but-extrapolatable (we forecast, we plan, we model). This burger's move is to flip the future's status: hold it not as extrapolation but as *tehom* — the Hebrew primordial deep, formless and unknowable, the category the past already occupies for anyone who takes ancestry, reincarnation, or genuine historical humility seriously.

## Why it changes anything

The felt experience of decision-making changes when the future is held as tehom rather than as forecast. The forecast stance sees a decision as choosing between predicted outcomes. The tehom stance sees a decision as choosing which direction to face when the unknowable arrives. Different problem. Different discipline. Different failure modes.

The forecast stance's failure mode is confidence in the projection — mistaking the model for the territory. The tehom stance's failure mode is paralysis — mistaking unknowability for unactionability. Neither is the healthy position by default. The interesting move is knowing when to use which.
