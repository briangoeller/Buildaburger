## The model

Self-model of cognition. Four regions:

- **Answer-ready** — things the mind produces on demand. Cached responses, memorized facts, worn conclusions.
- **Computing** — things being actively worked on. Problems mid-solve, sentences mid-form, decisions in progress.
- **Unanswerable** — things the mind knows it cannot answer and has learned to name as such. Foundational metaphysical questions, some kinds of long-horizon predictions, most questions about other minds.
- **Question-generating** — the region from which new questions arise. Not answers-in-waiting; questions the mind has not yet formed. *Tehom.*

## The observation worth naming

The fourth region is felt as *presence*, not as absence-of-answer, not as a to-do queue. A continuous background awareness of something vast and unstructured, weighted, and — historically for me — burdensome. In coaching work last year the fourth region was reframed as gift rather than burden, and the reframe took. Now the fourth region is where I go for something rather than the room I'm trying to leave.

That is the whole observation. It is small. It is also load-bearing for a lot of how I organize the rest of what I do.

## State of play

Public per the site's therapeutic-offloading rationale, and I want to be honest that this one is on the edge of what belongs on a public index. It describes the interior architecture of my own cognition and is more vulnerable than the other raw burgers, which are external-facing speculations. If a reader is reading it, the reader is welcome. The burger isn't trying to convert anyone to the model. It's here so that the question-generating region is *named*, and named is a different kind of held than unnamed.
