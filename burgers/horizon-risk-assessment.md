## The idea

An eight-dimension scoring instrument for community bank survival risk over a ten-year horizon. The dimensions cover the actual existential pressures, not the categories a CAMELS-shaped world risk-rates by default: AI disruption to the labor and product surface, the GENIUS Act stablecoin disintermediation pathway, employment shock in the bank's geographic footprint, the demographic cliff and what it does to a deposit base, cyber, regulatory drift, talent, and consolidation pressure.

The point isn't to produce a number. The point is to make the board see all eight at once.

## What came together

A self-contained HTML scoring tool with Export/Import JSON so a scoring session can be saved and revisited. No consultant required to sit in front of it — the instrument is designed to be operated by the executive team itself, which is how it ends up being used honestly rather than performatively.

The rename from *Strategic Threat Assessment* to *Horizon Risk Assessment* wasn't cosmetic. The earlier framing read as intelligence-shop vocabulary and triggered the reflex to push back before the first dimension was scored. The new framing reads as established executive-team language and lets the conversation start.

## State of play

The instrument is shipped. The next move is engagement one: scoring a bank with live data, which requires pulling deposit-by-age data and footprint employment-by-sector data — both gettable, neither in hand yet.

The broader use is the productized version. This is the load-bearing artifact for fractional advisory work with other community banks on AI risk and strategic positioning. The instrument had to exist before that conversation could be sold. Now it does.
