## The idea

Peter Turchin's structural-demographic theory models societies as running through long macrocycles of integration and disintegration, driven by four measurable variables — elite overproduction, popular immiseration, state fiscal distress, and geopolitical position. His methodology scores historical periods on a composite scale, with disintegrative crises appearing at recognizable thresholds. Turchin has been public since 2010 that the U.S. was heading into one, dated for the early-to-mid 2020s.

The macrocycle hypothesis takes that framework, applies it to the 2020s data, and asks a specific question: **which prior American period is the true structural twin of the current one?** The received narrative points to 1970 — cultural turmoil, monetary shocks, foreign policy failure. On Turchin's variables, 1970 scores 2.87. 2025 scores 8.03. They're not the same phenomenon at all.

The structural twin of 2025 is 1900, which scores 6.67 — the last time American elite overproduction and popular immiseration coincided at Progressive-Era magnitudes. That period ended in the reform wave of 1900–1920 (Progressive movement, trust-busting, direct election of senators, income tax, women's suffrage), not in revolutionary rupture. The macrocycle hypothesis says the 2020s are likely to end similarly *if certain conditions hold* — and the master variable governing which side of the ledger it lands on is what I've come to call the **non-kinetic export valve**.

## The non-kinetic export valve

Every high-cycle period has an accumulated pool of internal tensions — resentments, dislocations, generational conflicts, class friction — that has to go somewhere. The question isn't whether the tensions will be released but through what channel. Historically, elites have three options for offloading them:

- **Foreign kinetic** — war, colonial adventurism, military mobilization. Historically the most common, but expensive and often catastrophically counterproductive at the endgame.
- **Domestic reform** — the Progressive playbook. Absorb the tensions by delivering visible concessions (regulation of monopolies, expansion of the franchise, redistributive taxation). Costly to elites but survivable.
- **Non-kinetic external** — trade friction, migration policy, cultural diplomacy, information warfare, economic sanctions. The "vent-without-shooting" channel that operates through economic and cultural pressure rather than military engagement.

The hypothesis is that the openness of the non-kinetic external channel is the master variable determining whether a high-cycle period resolves in the 1900 pattern (reform) or the 1930s pattern (rupture). A wide non-kinetic external valve absorbs enough internal pressure to buy time for reform. A closed non-kinetic valve — universal tariffs, closed borders, breakdown of international economic order — forces the pressure to release through either the foreign-kinetic channel or the domestic-rupture channel.

## Lifeboat Capitalism and the Tiering

Two adjacent framings for the same underlying phenomenon.

**Lifeboat Capitalism** — the mode where elite strategy shifts from growing the pie to hardening one's share against the coming disintegration. Bunker construction, sovereign-alternative citizenships, self-sufficient estates, private security. Historically observable in every prior high-cycle period; visible now in the residential real estate market in places like Bozeman, Franklin (TN), and post-COVID New Zealand. Not a conspiracy — a rational individual response to a shared assessment of macrocycle position. The problem is that Lifeboat Capitalism as a collective behavior *accelerates* the disintegration it's trying to hedge against, because the withdrawal of elite investment in shared institutions is one of the primary mechanisms Turchin's model tracks.

**The Tiering** — the observation that Lifeboat Capitalism produces a stratified society not along traditional class lines but along *institutional participation* lines. Tier 1 lives inside the shared institutions and depends on them. Tier 2 lives adjacent to them, uses them selectively, and hedges its exposure. Tier 3 has effectively exited — the ultra-wealthy who no longer require police protection, public schools, currency stability, or federal courts. The Tiering matters because Turchin's model measures elite defection from shared institutions as one of the leading indicators of disintegration, and the Tiering is what elite defection looks like on the ground in 2025.

## State of play

The framework exists as a scored hypothesis with named framings and a master-variable claim. It is not written up. The immediate publishing move is a Substack-length essay establishing the 1900-not-1970 structural-twin claim, using the Turchin scores directly and citing the four variables individually to keep the argument concrete. The longer treatment — Lifeboat Capitalism, the Tiering, and the non-kinetic export valve as a policy hinge — is book-shaped rather than essay-shaped and belongs downstream of the Substack essay.

The hypothesis has a genuine failure mode I want to be honest about: Turchin's variables are causally suggestive but not predictively decisive. Two societies with identical scores can resolve differently. The framework is best read as a diagnostic for where in the arc a society sits, not as a prediction of which endgame it takes. The 1900-versus-1930 forkchoice remains a function of decisions being made now, not a settled destination.
