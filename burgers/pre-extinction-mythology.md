## The premise

A mythology project rather than a novel.

Extinction cycles on Earth are periodic — this is settled paleontology, with characteristic intervals for the major and mass events. What if the dying civilizations of prior cycles encoded messages intended for the next? What would the messages need to say to be worth encoding? What geological media would have to be chosen for them to survive?

## The source materials

*Tehom* (Hebrew primordial deep, the formless waters of Genesis before creation). The Emerald Tablet (Hermetic-alchemical text of uncertain age, allegedly summarizing an older tradition). Rabbinic cosmology's treatment of pre-creation states.

These are chosen because they *already* have the texture of a message-from-a-lost-civilization surviving into a subsequent one, which suggests they may in fact be exactly that — or descended from something like it. The mythology's own frame turned in on itself. The device the project uses is the same device the project is depicting.

## The exercise

The value isn't in producing a document. The value is in designing what such a document would have to say to be worth carrying across a mass extinction. That constraint forces a specific discipline: what is worth preserving when you can preserve very little, and when the audience is not you, not your descendants, and not culturally continuous with you? The answers to that question are probably more interesting than the mythology they would build.

## State of play

Raw. Adjacent to the Siderophile burger — both are about traces surviving the boundary layer, at different scales again. Also adjacent to the Backward-Facing Observer and Four-Space Cognition Model burgers via the shared treatment of tehom as a real category. All four might belong in a single longer treatment eventually. Not yet.
