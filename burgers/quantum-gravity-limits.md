## The question

Why has quantum mechanics never been reconciled with general relativity? Three candidate explanations live inside that one question, and they fail or succeed for different reasons:

1. **The wrong toolkit.** Our existing mathematical landscape doesn't fit the problem, but the right mathematics is reachable inside our axiomatic foundations — we just haven't found it yet. This is the mainstream working assumption and the inductively well-supported default.
2. **An axiomatic limit.** Some Gödel-adjacent reading where number theory or set theory themselves cannot contain the required structure. *This one tends to rest on a misreading of what Gödel proved* — incompleteness is about provability within a formal system, not about physical representability. Worth flagging but probably not the answer.
3. **Not modelable at all.** The universe contains structure that no formal system can express, even with new axioms. The strongest version of this claim, also the most expensive to defend.

## What got sharpened

The interesting move was sideways. The conversation produced a candidate Popperian experiment — **Design 1**, a calorimetry test of substrate cost during measurement events — that converts a speculative two-mode-universe framing into a number an experimentalist could in principle plot.

The unresolved load-bearing wall: an operational definition of *rendering* that distinguishes an observer from a warm rock without smuggling consciousness in as a primitive. Until that's defined, Designs 2 and 3 aren't experiments. Design 1 doesn't need it.

## State of play

Raw on purpose. The valuable thing here isn't a position, it's that the speculation got *sharper* under pressure rather than dissolving. Worth a follow-up that takes Design 1 to people doing quantum thermodynamics work — they're measuring energy ledgers across measurement events right now, just not asking this question of the data.

A speculation that can be *ended* by a graph is in better shape than one that can't. This one has that property, almost uniquely among the cosmological pieces in the pile.
