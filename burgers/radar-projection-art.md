## The piece

A vintage box camera on a wooden surveyor's tripod. The camera is a housing — a pico projector lives inside it, aimed downward. On the ground, a 1931 paper map of Wisconsin. The projector displays live NOAA radar imagery onto the map.

The 1931 map is old enough that the geography it shows isn't quite the current one. Towns that have since disappeared are on it. Roads that no longer exist are on it. Current towns aren't. So the current weather is being projected onto a past geography, and the mismatch is the piece — the storm is real, but the state it's crossing is the state as it was ninety-five years ago.

## State of play

Physical build in progress. The vintage box camera has been sourced. The tripod exists. The projector is spec'd but not purchased. Nothing is mounted yet. Not conceptually stuck — just not built yet. The interesting engineering decision left to make is whether the box camera housing should mask the projector completely or partially expose the mechanism, and that's really an aesthetic call, not a technical one.
