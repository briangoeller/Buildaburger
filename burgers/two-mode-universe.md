## The speculation

Physical reality runs in two modes. **Unrendered**: quantum superposition, cheap, high-potential, no substrate cost. **Rendered**: classical reality, decoherence genuinely resolved (rather than merely spread), substrate cost paid. Dark matter is the observable signature of the substrate that pays the rendering cost — not produced by observation but the medium observation runs in. We live inside a data center.

The sharp version of the claim is that observed cosmic history is partly *constructed* by the act of doing cosmology, in the way a consistent-histories interpretation of quantum mechanics permits. We didn't discover the CMB; we rendered it, and the universe generated a 13.8-billion-year backstory consistent with the observation.

## Where it breaks

The load-bearing problem is the causality direction. Dark matter halos predate intelligent life by billions of years. So intelligent observation can't be *causing* the substrate that enabled the observers. Retrocausality could rescue it, but retrocausality is a wilder speculation than the one it would be rescuing, so the fix is worse than the problem.

Two other pressures worth naming: no principled definition of *observation* that privileges consciousness without becoming special pleading, and an entropy accounting that runs backwards under the standard second-law reading.

## Where it might partially survive

A Wolfram-hypergraph reading gives the *verb* "render" a mechanism: the observer sequences a multiway graph into a single branchial thread, and that sequencing is the rendering. What Wolfram doesn't provide — and won't — is the *cost accounting*. The hypergraph has no thermodynamics of observation; the substrate updates at the same rate whether an observer's thread passes through or not.

So the survivable version of the speculation is: rendering-as-sequencing, no cost, no dark matter connection. Which is a much less interesting speculation than the original, but a more honest one.

## State of play

Raw on purpose. Overlaps with the Quantum Gravity & the Limits of Mathematics burger — both are trying to name a boundary the standard model isn't naming, and this one is the more speculative half of that pair. Held here as a thing to keep noticing rather than a thing to write up.
